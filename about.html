About Me

My Story

I first started my journey into the world of User Experience during my master’s degree in game design, where I took classes in various social research methods, project management, quality assurance and usability testing. During my degree I worked at an external games user research lab where I worked on a variety of projects for many AAA video game industry clients.

The purpose behind my work and the benefit I was able to provide for my clients created a very strong attachment within me to the process of user experience research. I felt that if every company (especially the government, I’m looking at you DMV) could better understand its users and create products to better serve their needs that this world we live in would be a much better place.

After my degree I decided I wanted to be able to create my own experiences and not just enhance another’s so I started the UX Design Certification course at Career Foundry where I learned the skills and gained experience with the tools I would need to be both a researcher and designer. With my new skills in design I am working on projects to better serve people in my community by partnering with a local community outreach center to revamp their website and develop a mobile application aimed at aiding those looking for help and those looking to help by better organizing and presenting resources that are already available.
